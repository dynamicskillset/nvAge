## Design Context

### Users
General audience who want a simple, fast notes app. Not necessarily technical — they want something that "just works" like Notational Velocity but feels refined and approachable. The job is quick capture and instant retrieval of thoughts, ideas, and notes. The interface should evoke calm confidence — no friction, no learning curve, just type and find.

### Brand Personality
**Refined, modern, calm.** nvAge should feel like a well-crafted tool — not utilitarian to the point of coldness, but not decorative either. Think Bear Notes or iA Writer: clean, polished, warm. Privacy-first and local-first values mean the app should feel trustworthy and grounded.

**Three words:** Calm, focused, trustworthy.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Nord color palette** — the arctic, north-bluish palette from nordtheme.com. This is the established design language across the dynamicskillset ecosystem (used in Stream, TaskDial, and other projects).

- **Polar Night** (`#2e3440`, `#3b4252`, `#434c5e`, `#4c566a`) — backgrounds and surfaces
- **Snow Storm** (`#d8dee9`, `#e5e9f0`, `#eceff4`) — text and primary content
- **Frost** (`#8fbcbb`, `#88c0d0`, `#81a1c1`, `#5e81ac`) — accents and interactive elements
- **Aurora** (`#bf616a`, `#d08770`, `#ebcb8b`, `#a3be8c`, `#b48ead`) — semantic colors (errors, warnings, success)

**Anti-references:** Generic dark-mode-with-blue-accent AI slop. Notion/Obsidian chrome-heavy PKM apps. Anything that looks like a SaaS dashboard.

**Theme:** Dark mode using Nord's Polar Night palette. The current `#0a0a0a` background and `#3b82f6` blue accent are generic and should be replaced with Nord's cohesive arctic palette.

### Design Principles
1. **Calm over clever** — the interface should disappear and let the notes be the focus
2. **Nord is the language** — every color choice comes from the Nord palette, no arbitrary hex values
3. **Keyboard-first, mouse-friendly** — power users navigate without leaving the keyboard, but nothing is hidden from mouse users
4. **Zero chrome** — minimal UI decoration; every pixel earns its place
5. **Consistent with the ecosystem** — should feel like it belongs alongside Stream, TaskDial, and other dynamicskillset apps
